## Mastering the Superstar Baseball Power Stats Guide: Frame Data

To truly excel, you must understand **Batting Frames**. In Superstar Baseball, a swing isn't a single animation; it is a series of contact windows. Most charge hits occur between **Frame 9 and Frame 3**. The lower the frame number, the "later" the contact. 

### Understanding Advanced Contact Metrics
Competitive players now track three primary statistics to determine a player's efficiency:
1.  **CHP (Contact Hit Percentage):** Your hits divided by total contact (hits + fouls + outs).
2.  **CTBA (Contact Total Batting Average):** Hits divided by (hits + outs), excluding fouls.
3.  **CHRP (Contact Home Run Percentage):** The percentage of times your contact results in a home run.

| Contact Type | Frame Window | Strategy | Result |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Early** | 9 - 7 | Pull the ball | High Power, High Foul Risk |
| **Perfect** | 6 | Center-cut | Maximum Exit Velocity |
| **Late** | 5 - 3 | Push the ball | Higher Contact Average (CHP) |

As shown in our **superstar baseball power stats guide**, aiming for Frame 4 or 5 is often more beneficial for "Curve Trajectory" hitters. Being slightly late allows the ball to travel deeper into the zone, often resulting in "Push Hits" that stay fair along the lines, whereas being too early on Frame 9 frequently leads to foul-ball strikeouts.

## Pitching Strategy: Pattern Recognition

Power hitting is only half the battle. To win consistently, you must shut down your opponent's power hitters using the "Fake Ball" and location-based pitching.

1.  **The Low Fastball Spam:** Most players struggle with vertical aim. By "spamming" fastballs to the bottom corners of the strike zone, you force the batter to "under-cut" the ball perfectly to get a hit.
2.  **The Fake Ball:** If your pitcher has a "Fake Ball" style, use it sparingly. The key is to watch for the "Anime Aura" tell. If you activate it every time, experienced batters will simply wait for the real ball to appear.
3.  **The Sinker/Change-up Mix:** Use the Sinker when you have two strikes. Its sharp vertical drop often induces "Weak" contact sounds, leading to easy ground outs.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Avoid throwing repeated Change-ups to "Competitive Sweats." High-level players will recognize the slow velocity and time a "nuke" swing, resulting in an immediate home run.

## Advanced Fielding and Shortcuts

Defense in Superstar Baseball is often won in the milliseconds between catching and throwing. Utilizing keyboard shortcuts (if playing on PC) or precise button inputs is mandatory.

*   **The "1" Key:** Immediately throws to first base. This should be your muscle-memory reaction for any ground ball to the infield.
*   **The "Monkey in the Middle":** If a runner is caught between bases, don't keep throwing the ball back and forth. The "slow throw" mechanic in 2026 makes this risky. Instead, use your player's avatar to chase them down physically.
*   **Diving:** Use the Space or Shift key to stop balls that are just out of reach. A successful dive can negate a high-power "Combustion" hit that would otherwise reach the gap.
