## Superstar Baseball All Positions Guide: Roster Breakdown

In the current meta, there are 11 distinct positions you must fill to have a complete, competitive team. These are split between eight batting/fielding positions and three specialized pitching roles. Understanding the stat priorities for each is the first step toward efficient training.

### Batter Positions and Stat Priorities

For batters, the four primary stats—Strength (STR), Dexterity (DEX), Intelligence (INT), and Mental (MNT)—play different roles depending on where the player stands on the field. While every hitter wants high Strength for home runs, a Shortstop needs significantly more Dexterity than a First Baseman.

| Position | Primary Stat | Secondary Stat | Defensive Focus |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Catcher (C)** | **Strength** | Mental | High Fielding/Arm |
| **First Base (1B)** | **Strength** | Intelligence | Power Hitting |
| **Second Base (2B)** | **Dexterity** | Strength | Speed/Agility |
| **Third Base (3B)** | **Strength** | Dexterity | Strong Arm |
| **Shortstop (SS)** | **Dexterity** | Intelligence | Maximum Range |
| **Left Field (LF)** | **Strength** | Dexterity | Balanced |
| **Center Field (CF)** | **Dexterity** | Strength | High Speed |
| **Right Field (RF)** | **Strength** | Dexterity | Strong Arm |

### Pitcher Roles and Specializations

Pitchers are divided into three categories. In the "My Player" mode, training a pitcher generally takes longer (roughly 90 minutes compared to 60 minutes for a batter), so choosing the right role before you start is crucial.

| Pitcher Role | Description | Key Performance Indicator |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Starting Pitcher (SP)** | The backbone of your rotation; requires high stamina. | Innings Pitched / ERA |
| **Relief Pitcher (RP)** | Mid-game specialists used to bridge the gap to the end. | Hold Percentage |
| **Closing Pitcher (CP)** | High-velocity specialists designed to shut down the 9th inning. | Save Percentage |

> **💡 Tip:** When starting out, aim to develop at least 10 batters and 10 pitchers across various banners to unlock the maximum number of trainer pulls and rewards.

## Offensive Styles and Hitting Mechanics

Positioning is only half the battle; how your players perform at the plate determines your climb through the Superstar League. When developing your batters, you must select an offensive style that complements their position and stat build.

### Power vs. Contact Styles

If you are training a corner infielder (1B/3B) or a Catcher, you should prioritize styles that buff power. The **superstar baseball all positions guide** recommends the following styles for maximum home run potential:

*   **Impact:** Considered the best for raw power, though it requires precise timing.
*   **Combustion:** Highly recommended for 2026 because it offers a significant power buff while being easier to master than Impact.
*   **Heat:** A solid alternative that balances power with a slight increase in the hitting zone.

To hit "insane" home runs, timing and coordination are paramount. You want the ball to be centered in your hitting reticle at the exact moment of contact. Using a keyboard and mouse via an emulator can often make this coordination easier for players struggling with touch controls.

## The Role of Trainers and Cores

Advanced players use this **superstar baseball all positions guide** to map out their Core upgrades and Trainer levels. Trainers provide the base stats and skills during the "My Player" mode, while Cores provide massive flat stat boosts to the finished player.

### Maximizing Core Stats

Cores are obtained primarily through the Planet League. It is better to play on "Hard" difficulty and lose than to play on "Normal" and win every game, as the Core drops on Hard are significantly superior. A single high-ranked player can carry over 200+ additional stats just from their equipped Cores.

### Trainer Cubing and Diamonds

To afford the 10+1 pulls needed to get UR trainers, you need a steady stream of Diamonds. One of the best "hidden" ways to get Diamonds is through trainer development:
1.  Level up your **SR (Super Rare)** trainers to Rank 5.
2.  Each time you hit Rank 5 with an SR trainer, you receive roughly 180 Diamonds via achievements.
3.  With over 40 SR trainers available, this method can net you over 7,000 Diamonds for free.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not "cube" or sacrifice your rare trainers until they have reached Rank 5. This ensures you maximize the achievement rewards before recycling the trainer for experience chips.

## FAQ

**Q: Which position should I train first as a beginner?**

A: This **superstar baseball all positions guide** suggests training at least 10 batters and 10 pitchers across the Gameville and Cruel Pumas banners first. This allows you to unlock early UR tickets and build a foundation of trainers before attempting the harder banners like Mystic Unicorns.

**Q: How many positions are there in Superstar Baseball?**

A: There are 11 total positions: 8 for batters/fielders (C, 1B, 2B, 3B, SS, LF, CF, RF) and 3 for pitchers (SP, RP, CP). You should aim to have at least one high-ranked player for each spot.

**Q: What is the best way to get UR trainers?**

A: The most reliable way is completing the 11 challenges in each My Player banner. Each completed banner set grants a guaranteed UR ticket. Additionally, grinding the "My Player" mode to level up your account rewards you with Diamonds for 10+1 pulls.

**Q: Does the school/banner affect the player's final stats?**

A: Yes. For example, River City High is known for producing some of the highest-rated players but has much harder challenges. Beginners should start with Gameville to learn the mechanics before moving to high-stat banners.
