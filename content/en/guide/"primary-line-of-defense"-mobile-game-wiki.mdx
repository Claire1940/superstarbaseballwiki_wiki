## The 90-Mob Economy Snowball Strategy

One of the most effective ways to jumpstart your economy in Lucky Defense is the "90-mob quest" strategy. This technique focuses on maximizing early-game coin generation to fund your **primary line of defense** before the waves become unmanageable.

In the early stages, a specific quest rewards players with 200 coins once the total number of mobs on the track reaches 90. While 200 coins might seem insignificant in the mid-game, receiving them within the first few waves allows you to spawn multiple units simultaneously, creating an immediate power spike.

| Step | Action | Timing/Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | Communicate with Teammate | Wave 1 Start |
| **2** | AFK (Do Not Spawn) | Until 80+ Mobs |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not wait until the counter hits exactly 90. If you or your teammate experience lag, the mob count may exceed the limit, causing you to lose energy and fail the run before it even begins. Spawning at 87 mobs provides a safe buffer.

## Categorizing Your Primary Line of Defense

To build a balanced board, you must understand the specialized roles of your Guardians. Relying solely on one type of unit—such as having only boss killers—will lead to a quick defeat when faced with high-density mob waves.

### Guardian Roles and Synergies
A successful **primary line of defense** consists of three core roles: AOE Damage Dealers, Boss Killers, and Support/Stun units.

1.  **AOE Damage Dealers:** Units like **Batman** excel at clearing large groups of standard mobs. They prevent the track from becoming overwhelmed.
2.  **Boss Killers:** Guardians like **Lancelot** are essential for Wave 10, 20, and beyond. Lancelot, specifically when upgraded with Luck Stones, can deal damage based on a percentage of the boss's max health.
3.  **Support & Utility:** Units like **Kitty Mage** provide Mana generation, while **Coldi** offers both slows and stuns.

| Guardian | Role | Key Benefit |

## The Importance of Stun Locking

In the later stages of the game, damage alone is rarely enough. You must implement crowd control (CC) to keep enemies within the "Kill Chamber"—the specific area where your highest DPS units can hit them. Any reliable **primary line of defense** mobile game wiki will emphasize that stuns are the number one factor in beating late-game bosses.

### Top Stun Units Comparison
| Unit Type | Range | Stun Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| **Electro Robot** | Melee | Frequent (Short Duration) |
| **Shock Robot** | Ranged | Occasional (Long Distance) |
| **Coldi (Mythic)** | Large AOE | Constant Slow + Stun |

Strategic placement is vital here. Place your melee stun units (Electro Robots) directly adjacent to the track. Ranged stun units (Shock Robots) should be placed in the middle of the board. This prevents them from stunning mobs too early on the left side of the track, which can actually be detrimental if you want to group enemies up for AOE attacks.

## Mythic Summoning: Efficiency vs. Waste

Summoning Mythics is a high-stakes gamble. While it is tempting to summon every Mythic as soon as the recipe is available, doing so can deplete the resources needed for superior units. For example, the **Ninja** Mythic is often considered a "trap" because it requires units like the Wolf Warrior and Satan, which are better utilized for the **Frog Prince** or **Paladin** recipes.

> **💡 Tip:** Always check your "Guardian Recipes" before merging. If a Mythic doesn't solve a current hole in your defense (e.g., you need stuns but the Mythic is pure DPS), it is better to keep the component units for their passive buffs.

### Passive Buff Units
Some units provide global benefits just by existing on your board:
*   **Eagle General:** Grants a **10% attack speed** boost per Eagle General spawned.
*   **Wolf Warrior:** Provides a **10% attack damage** increase to all units.

## Resource Management and Gambling

Lucky Defense uses two primary currencies: Coins and Luck Stones. While coins are used for basic summons, Luck Stones are the "premium" in-match currency used for upgrading Mythics and gambling for higher-tier units.

According to advanced **primary line of defense** strategies, you should avoid gambling your Luck Stones on the "Legendary" slot. The 10% success rate is statistically poor compared to the "Epic" slot. It is much safer to build your legendaries through consistent Epic pulls and manual merges.

If you find yourself short on resources, remember that you can sell unwanted units. Selling a Rare or Epic unit can refund Luck Stones, which might be exactly what you need to upgrade a Lancelot right before a major boss wave.

## Strategic Board Placement

The layout of your board determines how much "uptime" your damage dealers have on the enemies. A common mistake is spreading units out evenly. Instead, you should create a concentrated zone of destruction.

1.  **Top-Left Corner:** Place your highest AOE DPS here. This allows them to hit mobs as soon as they enter and as they loop around.
2.  **Kill Chamber:** Position your Boss Killers (Lancelot) one tile to the right of your main DPS.
3.  **The Stun Zone:** Place melee stunners directly next to your Boss Killers to ensure the boss stays in their range for as long as possible.

You can find more detailed placement grids on the [official Lucky Defense community pages](https://discord.com/invite/luckydefense) to optimize your specific unit composition.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the best way to use Luck Stones in the early game?**

A: Save your Luck Stones for upgrading high-impact Mythics like Lancelot or Frog Prince. Avoid gambling for Legendaries unless your **primary line of defense** is already stable and you have a surplus of stones.

**Q: Why should I AFK at the start of a match?**

A: AFKing until the mob count reaches 90 triggers a hidden quest that rewards 200 coins. This allows you to build a much stronger economy than players who spawn units immediately.

**Q: Which Mythic unit is the best for clearing Wave 80?**

A: The Frog Prince (when transformed into King Dian) is widely considered the strongest unit for late-game clears due to its massive scaling damage. Lancelot is also top-tier for his percentage-based health damage against bosses.

**Q: How many stun units do I need for a solid primary line of defense?**

A: Most high-level players recommend having at least two to three dedicated stun units (like Electro Robots or Coldi) to ensure that bosses remain stationary in your high-damage zones.
