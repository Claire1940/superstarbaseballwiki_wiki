## The Foundation: Owner's Guide and Basic Manual

Before diving into the deep grind, every player must clear the initial manuals. These serve as a tutorial but offer some of the most concentrated rewards in the game. Completing the Basic Manual is non-negotiable for a healthy start.

| Manual Task | Primary Reward | Significance |
|---|---|---|
| **Complete Basic Manual** | Guaranteed UR Trainer | Essential for team power scaling |
| **First Recruitment** | 90% Guaranteed Bar Boost | Sets up a UR within 10 pulls |
| **Owner's Guide Missions** | 400+ Diamonds | Provides currency for the first 10+1 pull |
| **Intensive Manual** | Experience Chips | Used to level up your core trainers |

> **💡 Tip:** Do not rush into the "Intensive Manual" immediately. Focus on getting your first UR and 10+1 pull to build a baseline deck before tackling the harder development tasks.

## My Player Mode: The Achievement Engine

The heart of any **superstar baseball achievement guide** lies in the My Player mode. To maximize your efficiency in 2026, you should stop trying to build the "perfect" player right away. Instead, focus on volume. The game rewards you for the total number of players developed, regardless of their final rank.

### The 20-Player Rotation
To unlock the maximum rewards for each school (Gamevil, Cruel Pumas, Shining Angels, River City High, and Mystic Unicorns), you should aim to train at least 20 players per banner. This strategy triggers multiple tier-based and volume-based achievements simultaneously.

1.  **Passive Training:** Use the "Auto" and "Saving Mode" features while multitasking. A single run takes 15–20 minutes.
2.  **Positional Diversity:** Train 10 batters and 10 pitchers for each school.
3.  **Stat Variation:** Ensure you are developing players with different primary stats (Strength, Intelligence, Dexterity, and Mental) to hit the "Develop 100 Strength Players" type milestones.

| Achievement Category | Target Goal | Reward Type |
|---|---|---|
| **Positional Mastery** | 30 Players per Position | Gold and Achievement Points |
| **Stat Specialization** | 175 Dexterity Players | Diamond Bundles |
| **Banner Completion** | 20 Players per School | SS/SSR Tickets & Diamonds |
| **Overall Level** | Reach Level 4000+ | Large Diamond Milestones (e.g., 500) |

## Superstar League and Performance Milestones

Once you have a functional team, the Superstar League becomes your primary source of recurring income. In 2026, the gap between ranks is significant, but the "one-time" achievement rewards for specific feats are accessible even to mid-tier teams.

### High-Value League Achievements
Recording specific statistics in a single game or over a season can net you hundreds of Diamonds. These are often overlooked by players who only focus on winning the match.

*   **No-Hitter:** Recording a no-hitter in Superstar League grants 200 Diamonds.
*   **Home Run King:** Hitting 7+ home runs in a single game provides 150 Diamonds.
*   **Strikeout Machine:** Recording 18+ strikeouts in a single game yields 200 Diamonds.
*   **High Scoring:** Scoring 20+ runs in a single game rewards 200 Diamonds.

> **💡 Tip:** Use a "Keyboard and Mouse" setup via an emulator if possible; it significantly improves hitting precision for the Home Run King achievements.

## Trainer Development and Diamond Farming

One of the "pro secrets" in any **superstar baseball achievement guide** is the Trainer Rank-Up trick. Many players ignore their Super Rare (SR) trainers in favor of URs, but SRs are actually a gold mine for Diamonds.

Every time you take an SR trainer from Rank 1 to Rank 5, you earn approximately 180 Diamonds through the collection and development missions. If you manage to rank up 40+ SR trainers over your career, you can generate over 7,000 Diamonds.

### The Cubing Process
Once a trainer reaches Rank 5, do not simply let them sit there.
1.  **Max Rank:** Hit Rank 5 to claim the Diamond achievement.
2.  **Cubing:** Use the "Cubing" feature to break down Rare and Super Rare trainers.
3.  **Reward:** This yields Rare and Super Rare boxes containing Experience Chips and Trainer Genes, which are essential for further progression.

## Advanced Progression: Planet League and Cores

By mid-2026, your focus should shift to Planet League. The difficulty spike in "Hard" mode is worth the struggle because of the Core drop rates. Cores provide massive stat boosts that make high-tier achievements (like developing an SSS-ranked player) possible.

| Core Tier | Typical Stat Boost | Preferred Mode |
|---|---|---|
| **Normal/Rare** | +10 to +30 | Easy/Normal |
| **Super Rare** | +40 to +80 | Hard (Earth/Dragonica) |
| **Ultra Rare** | +100+ and Affinity | Hard (Legend Ranks) |

Even if your team loses most games on "Hard" mode, the 2–5 Cores you receive per game are more valuable than the rewards for winning on "Normal." Equipping a full set of high-level Cores can add over 300 points to a player's base stats before training even begins.

## Lecter’s Lab and Holograms

To complete the more obscure achievements, you must utilize Lecter’s Lab. This facility allows you to research specific training holograms.

*   **Hologram Requirement:** Many research tasks require you to have a specific number of developed batters or pitchers (e.g., "Have 50 Developed Pitchers").
*   **D-Point Refills:** If you run out of development points while grinding for achievements, you can use Bronze Holograms to refill up to 100 D-Points. This is much more efficient than spending 30 Diamonds for a refill.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the fastest way to get Diamonds for a 10+1 pull?**

A: The fastest method is to complete the 20 initial player trainings for the Gamevil and Cruel Pumas banners. This triggers volume achievements and provides free recruitment tickets alongside Diamond rewards. Following a **superstar baseball achievement guide** like this one ensures you don't miss the hidden milestones.

**Q: Why should I train 20 players for schools I don't like?**

A: Each school has a unique set of 11 challenges. Completing all 11 challenges for any school rewards you with a guaranteed UR Trainer ticket. Even if you don't use that school for your main team, the UR trainer you receive can be used in your deck to boost overall stats.

**Q: Is it worth buying the Super Pass for achievements?**

A: While the Super Pass provides a "Premium" track of rewards, it is not necessary for completing the main achievement list. The "Free" side of the pass still offers enough resources to help you reach the Level 4000 development milestone.

**Q: How do I unlock the "Legend" rank achievements in Superstar League?**

A: Reaching Legend requires a team rank of at least S- or higher. To achieve this, you must equip your players with high-level Cores from Planet League (Hard mode) and maximize your trainer deck skills to at least level 7 or 10.
