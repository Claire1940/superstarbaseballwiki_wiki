## Gameplay Mechanics: Hitting and Fielding

### Scoring in the New Environment
To succeed in the current version of the **superstar baseball ballpark**, players should focus on "inside-out" swinging. Pulling the ball for a home run is still possible—especially with the new 536-foot maximum distance capability—but it is no longer the only viable strategy.

1.  **Aim for the Gaps:** Use the expanded outfield to your advantage by aiming for the power alleys.
2.  **Infield Positioning:** Ensure your team has a dedicated shortstop and second baseman. The larger field means ground balls sneak through more often if the middle infield is left open.
3.  **Base Running:** Be aggressive on doubles. The extra distance from the wall to the infield gives runners more time to reach third base on deep hits.

## Defensive Strategies for the New Meta

Defense is often overlooked in baseball simulations, but in the 2026 **superstar baseball ballpark**, it is the difference between winning and losing. Since the field is larger, "dead zones" have appeared in the shallow outfield where bloop singles often land.

*   **The Shift:** Against power hitters, shift your outfielders deeper. In the new ballpark, a ball hit over an outfielder's head is almost always a triple.
*   **Cutoff Man:** Always use a cutoff man when throwing from the warning track. The increased distance to home plate makes direct throws inaccurate and slow.
*   **Pitching Inside:** To prevent hitters from exploiting the large gaps, pitchers should focus on inside fastballs to jam the batter, forcing weak pop-ups rather than deep drives.

> **Warning:** Leaving the infield unattended is a common mistake in the new update. Always ensure your players are stationed at their designated bases to prevent easy "inside-the-park" scenarios.
